About Me

And why my moto is to always be humble, strong, and empathetic.

My most valubale teachers in life have been error messages and a barbell..

The Code

When first learning to code, I struggled to understand and accept a process in which your first attempt probably won't work. As I continued to grapple with it, I had to accept that if it's not working, it's never the computer's fault. It's yours. The responsibility and the burdern of carrying your own mistakes taught me to look back and fix them before I could go forward and continue building. Error messages used to elicit a sense of anxiety and a feeling of self doubt, but now there's an urge for me to learn and grow on these mistakes. Code has taught me to fail fast, take responsibility, and try again. I don't know everything, nor will I ever, but I know I can do anything.

The barbell

Like coding, the barbell is strictly tough love. At the end of the day, you're accomplishing a lift for you. No matter how sore you are, how weak you are, or how tired you feel, load up the weights and get under the bar.
